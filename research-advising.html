Working with Undergraduates

Mentoring undergraduate researchers is one of the most rewarding parts of my work at Lafayette College. I supervise honors theses and work closely with students through Lafayette's EXCEL Scholars program and as research assistants. Several of these collaborations have grown into co-authored, peer-reviewed publications.

If you are a Lafayette student interested in empirical research in health, labor, or the economics of gender and the family, I am glad to talk. Students are best prepared to contribute after completing coursework in intermediate microeconomics and econometrics, and some familiarity with Stata, R, or Python is helpful.

Recommendation Letters

I am glad to write letters of recommendation for students applying to internships, jobs, academic programs, and graduate school. The strongest letters come when I know you well—through coursework, advising, or research—so please reach out early and provide your resume/CV, a statement of purpose or job description, and the relevant deadlines.
